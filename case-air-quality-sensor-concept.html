01

Digital blindness

We spend 90% of our lives indoors. We invest in ergonomic chairs, organic food, filtered water — but the one resource we consume every second, 24 hours a day, remains completely invisible. No data, no control, no action.

This is what we call "digital blindness": our homes and offices are black boxes where air quality is left to chance, not choice. The consequences are real and measurable — but they accumulate silently, day after day.

"You can't manage what you can't see. Our home is a black box where safety is determined by chance — not our choice."

02

The zone system

The core design question: how do you communicate air quality status across a room, without requiring the user to open an app or approach the device? The answer is a colour zone system — four states, each with an unambiguous meaning aligned to WHO and GOST safety thresholds.

Three key indicators (CO₂, TVOC, PM) each get a separate section of the device body lit by an LED disc. The outer ring colour tells you the status at a glance from anywhere in the room. The user can configure zone thresholds independently — the system adapts to individual sensitivity and local standards.

03

Industrial design

The device needed to feel at home in any interior — not like medical equipment or industrial hardware. The design brief: a device you'd want to put in your living room. The enclosure combines a vertical body with three LED indicator zones and a circular secondary display at the base. The form directly maps onto the information architecture: each zone of the body corresponds to one LED indicator.

–

Key design decisions

01 · Form

Vertical body + circular base

The elongated form maps directly onto three sensor zones. The round second display at the base holds ambient data (time, temperature, humidity) that doesn't require immediate attention — separating urgency from context.

02 · Colour panels

10 replaceable color panels

The decorative panels snap on and off without tools. 10 color options. This is "Design as Respect" — the device adapts to the user's space, not the other way around. A small accessory that transforms perceived value.

03 · Mounting

Wall-mount + desk stand

Standard electrical-box alignment means the device sits naturally next to a light switch — where users already look. The desk stand enables use on nightstands, desktops, and countertops with an adjustable angle.

04

The product line

Early feedback noted that not all users need a full display unit. This led to splitting the product into separate components — a sensor unit, a display unit, and an outdoor unit — purchasable independently or as a system. The result is a four-product line that covers every household scenario.

Flagship

AQ Pro

8 sensors, 2 IPS displays (1.9″ vertical + 1.8″ round), LED zone system, 10 replaceable color panels, wall-mount + desk stand.

Compact

AQ Lite

8 sensors, no display. Designed to stick under a tabletop or inside a cabinet. Sends data to Pro display unit or directly to the app.

Outdoor

AQ Street

IP65 outdoor unit. No CO₂, adds noise sensor. Answers: "Is it safe to open the window right now?" Works with Auto Drive.

Automation

Auto Drive

Wi-Fi window opener. Triggers automatically when CO₂ exceeds threshold. Works as a standalone "mini smart home" without a hub.

07

What I'd do differently

Start with modular hardware earlier. The original concept had a single all-in-one device. Community feedback revealed that many users only need the sensor, not the display. Splitting into AQ Pro / AQ Lite / AQ Street was right — but should have happened at the concept stage, not after publishing.

Document zone thresholds as a design artifact. The four-zone system is powerful, but threshold values need to be surfaced in the product, not buried in settings. Users need to understand why the zone changed, not just that it did.

The stand needed iteration from day one. The foldable stand concept (book-stand mechanism, adjustable angle) was identified late. For a device meant to travel to work and sit on a desk, portability is a core value — and the original fixed stand didn't reflect that.
